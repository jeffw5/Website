Value tension resolution — sample decision scenarios
Venue cost vs. accessibility
High-prestige venue strengthens reputation but raises attendance cost, limiting community reach.
Resolution guidancePreserve authority signal at flagship IS; offset access via hybrid/virtual track with reduced pricing or grant funding.
Open submissions vs. curated quality
Broad call for papers grows participation but may dilute technical rigor and IS reputation.
Resolution guidanceTier the program — peer-reviewed technical track for authority; practitioner/case study track for community growth.
Workshops vs. plenary scale
Deep workshops serve competency development but reduce session breadth and reach metrics.
Resolution guidanceMeasure outcomes per objective separately; workshops map to Obj 3, plenaries map to Obj 2 — both are valid IS investments.
Volunteer capacity vs. innovation
New format ideas (AI-assisted matching, virtual networking) strain volunteer bandwidth and risk quality.
Resolution guidancePilot innovations in bounded tracks before full deployment; operational excellence requires protecting core reliability first.
Priority when objectives conflict:
① Trusted authority ② Professional development ③ Community expansion ④ Operational excellence
